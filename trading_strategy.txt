1. MARKET CONDITIONS TO ENTER
------------------------------
- Stock price must be above the 200 MA on the weekly chart (trend direction filter)
- Stock price must be trading within 15% of the 20 MA on the weekly chart (mean-reversion proximity)
  Note: Previously 5% — loosened because a stock pulling back to a 10-day low can
  sit 10–15% below its 20 MA while still being in a healthy uptrend.
- Qualify the setup on the weekly chart first, then look for entry on the daily chart

2. ENTRY TRIGGERS
-----------------
Two separate setups:

Setup A — 10-Day Low Pullback:
  - Stock price pulls back to a 10-day closing low while above the weekly 200 MA
    Note: Previously 5-day — widened to generate more valid pullback entries.
  - Volume ranks in the top 20% over the last 12 weeks, sustained for 2 consecutive days
    Note: Previously 3 consecutive days — reduced because 3-day sustained elevated
    volume during a pullback is very rare in practice.

Setup B — 7% Drop with Hammer Confirmation:
  - Stock price drops 7% or more over the last 3 days
    Note: Previously 10% — lowered because a 10% 3-day drop almost never occurred
    in backtesting, making Setup B completely inactive.
  - Wait for a hammer candlestick pattern to form as confirmation before entering
  - Volume ranks in the top 20% over the last 12 weeks, sustained for 2 consecutive days

Volume Condition (both setups):
  - Volume must rank in the top 20% relative to the last 12 weeks
  - Elevated volume sustained for 2 consecutive days (previously 3)
  - Volume should not exceed the 10-day peak (avoid climax/exhaustion volume)

3. EXIT RULES
-------------
Take Profit:
  - Sell when the stock price reaches a clearly defined resistance level
    (60-day rolling high, excluding current bar to avoid lookahead)
  - Only take trades where resistance offers at least a 1.5:1 reward-to-risk ratio
    vs. the ATR stop loss distance
    Note: Previously 2:1 — lowered because the 60-day resistance target was
    frequently unreachable, filtering out too many valid setups.

Trailing Stop:
  - Once in profit, trail stop at 1.5 ATR below the current price (ratchet up only)
    Note: Previously 2 ATR — tightened to lock in gains faster.
  - The trailing stop only activates after holding for at least 10 days
    (grace period to avoid being stopped out during normal post-entry volatility)

MA Exit:
  - Exit when price closes below the 20 MA on the daily chart
  - This exit also only activates after holding for at least 10 days
    Note: Previously 5 days — extended so the strategy gives trades room to breathe
    before applying trend-exit rules.

4. STOP LOSS
------------
- Hard stop: If the stock price drops 2 ATR from the entry price, exit immediately
  Note: Previously 3 ATR — tightened so losses are cut faster, keeping avg loss
  closer to avg win size (improved profit factor from 1.13 → 1.30).
- This is dynamic and adjusts to current market volatility (ATR period: 14 days)

5. RISK MANAGEMENT
------------------
- Maximum 5 open positions at any time
- Maximum 15% of total capital per position
- Maximum 75% of capital deployed at once
- Keep a minimum 25% cash buffer for new opportunities or drawdown protection
- Minimum reward-to-risk ratio of 1.5:1 before entering any trade (previously 2:1)
